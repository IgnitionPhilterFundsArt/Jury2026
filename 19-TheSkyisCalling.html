19 - The Sky is Calling

Description for the Jury

The Vision" "The Sky is Calling" is an interactive, sound-responsive light installation designed to transform the auditory landscape of Flipside into a communal visual experience. The project consists of three 12-foot upright supports (structural bamboo) forming a 15-foot equilateral triangle. Suspended at varying heights within this footprint via high-tension airline cable is a "canopy" of six glowing halos. These "Rainbow Rings" range from 2" to 6" in diameter. The Interaction: The "Flipizen Experience"" This installation acts as a visual megaphone for the community. Using high-sensitivity sound sensors, the "Rainbow Rings" respond in real-time to the most radical of expressions. Whether a Flipizen claps, clucks, hoops, howls, or laughs, the rings ignite with cascading color and intensity. By rewarding unbridled vocal experimentation with a dazzling display of light, we create a feedback loop that invites every passerby to join in. Here, ONE becomes US in a shared chorus of cackles and cries. The social barriers that restrict us in the default world disappear, as individual noise inspires the collective into a light show for all. Technical & Safety Execution" To ensure the jury"s funds are used effectively and safely, we are utilizing: " High-Density COB LED Strips: Chosen for a "dot-free," seamless neon glow that looks professional and high-end. " Structural Integrity: The canopy is suspended using 1/8" stainless steel aircraft cable. The 12-foot poles are secured in weighted bases and anchored with heavy-duty earth augers to withstand Texas winds. " Safety & LNT: All guy-wires will be flagged with high-visibility neon ribbon and reflective tape for nighttime safety. The project is entirely "Plug-and-Play," meaning no on-site soldering or "micro-moop" creation. " Mitigation: Applying key insights from our previous large-scale builds, we have intentionally simplified the structural load and reduced mechanical complexity for this installation. We have implemented strict internal deadlines (including a full-scale dress rehearsal 10 days prior to the event) to ensure the piece is 'plug-and-play' and fully operational for the community by the first night of the burn.

Power Plan

The installation will run primarily on a high-capacity Battery/Inverter system to ensure silent operation and preserve the auditory environment for participant vocalization. An existing, owner-provided quiet inverter generator�housed in a noise-reducing baffle�will be used strictly as a backup or for recharging. Power is distributed via outdoor-rated extension cords protected by high-visibility cable covers. The 'Rainbow Rings' utilize a low-voltage DC system (12V/24V). All electronics and power supplies are housed in ventilated, IP65-rated weatherproof enclosures. While the rings are suspended 8 feet out of reach, the use of low-voltage components ensures the installation is inherently touch-safe. For added safety, an inline GFCI will be used at the source, and the backup generator will be placed on a secondary containment tray to ensure Leave No Trace (LNT) compliance.

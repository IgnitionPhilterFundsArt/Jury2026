14 - Liminal Lodge Field Lounge

Description for the Jury

The Liminal Lodge Field Lounge is an outdoor expansion of the Liminal Lodge, an immersive retro ranger field station brought to life last year with the support of the Ignition Philter art grant. The Lodge was designed as a liminal space, part art installation and part community refuge, where people could explore surreal interactive elements while also finding shade, water, fans, and a welcoming place to rest. This year, the primary focus is to enhance the Lodge"s curb appeal and create a larger, more inviting threshold that clearly signals what kind of world visitors are stepping into. The Field Lounge extends that vision outward, opening up the front of the installation so more Flipizens can be drawn in, linger, and connect before even entering the Lodge itself. The Field Lounge will be a semi screened patio built using a mix of structures we already own alongside high quality prefabricated canopies and screening products. By working smart with these modular systems, we can ensure reliable shade, airflow, and durability while keeping our energy focused on layered textures, set dressing, monumental signage, lighting, and storytelling elements that visually announce the identity of the space. These elements will frame the approach to the Lodge, making the experience feel intentional, immersive, and readable from the outside. For Flipizens, the Field Lounge is meant to be both an artwork and a living social space. Visitors can wander through the environment, discover playful details, interact with installations, or simply relax in the ambiance. The space will also host a free self service grab and go market with water and small comforts, and will serve as a flexible venue for community programming, including pop up dining, tarot readings, tea gatherings, and immersive interdimensional experiences planned by camp members. Ultimately, the Liminal Lodge Field Lounge invites people to explore, refuel, congregate, and enjoy a shared, imaginative place together.

Power Plan

We will power the installation using a mix of solar battery systems and a large gas generator dedicated to the installation. We are renting a golf cart and will have fuel on hand so our camp can easily check in, refuel, and keep things running throughout the event. Because the Lounge is located next to our camp, it will be simple for us to maintain and care for the power setup.

Alternative Budget Description

If awarded $575, we would focus grant funds on the essential pieces that make the Liminal Lodge Field Lounge work as a public art space: basic canopy structure, screening for shade and airflow, and the core lighting that defines the space at night. These elements are what make the patio safe, functional, and clearly readable as an intentional artwork rather than just camp infrastructure. To make this work at a reduced award, we would scale back on non-essential decorative items like extra rugs, textiles, props, and finer details on the neon sign, billboard, and CRT wall. Those aesthetic layers would be added later through self-funding from our camp. Water, hydration, and community giveaways would remain fully camp-funded as part of our gifting practice, and all grant dollars would be used strictly for building and developing the art space itself.

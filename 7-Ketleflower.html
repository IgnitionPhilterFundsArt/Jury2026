7 - Ketleflower

Description for the Jury

Ketleflower is a light and sound installation inspired by bioluminescence and the comfort of shared, restful space. The piece takes the form of a large sunflower, roughly twelve feet across including petals, built from lightweight materials and illuminated with individually addressable LEDs. It is paired with an immersive sound environment that blends original music with surround sound field recordings of natural environments from the region. Ketleflower got its start at Engulf where it has been installed and reinstalled over the course of several years, evolving each time through use, repair, and community feedback. Those outings shaped the piece into what it is now: less about spectacle, and more about offering a place where people can simply be together. At Flipside, Flipizens interact with Ketleflower by inhabiting the space it creates. People sit, lie down, cuddle, talk quietly, nap, or just breathe for a while within the light and sound. Some folks have deeply personal, reflective experiences, while others enjoy it casually as a cozy place to land, and both ways of engaging are completely valid. The piece asks very little of participants and gives them room to decide how they want to be with it. This proposal is for the original DIY version of Ketleflower. Grant funding would support transportation, setup and takedown help, routine repairs, and expanded seating and lounging options to make the space more comfortable and welcoming for y"all throughout the event. Ketleflower offers a gentle, grounding counterpoint to higher energy experiences on the playa. It"s a place to rest, reconnect, and share quiet moments together, shaped as much by the community as by the object itself.
